> (8%) Let $A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -2 & 3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -4 & 5 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -6 & 7 \end{bmatrix}$ and $B = (I+A)^{-1}(4I - A)$. Please calculate $(I+B)^{-1}$.
>
> 【108 中山通訊】

**解** $\because I + B = (I+A)^{-1}(I+A) + (I+A)^{-1}(4I-A) = (I+A)^{-1} \cdot 5I$

$$\therefore (I+B)^{-1} = \frac{1}{5}(I+A) = \frac{1}{5}\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -2 & 4 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -4 & 6 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -6 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$$
